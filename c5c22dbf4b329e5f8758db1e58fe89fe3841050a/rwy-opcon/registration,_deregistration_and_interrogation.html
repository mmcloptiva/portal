Registration, Deregistration and Interrogation

Description

Each employee can assign his personal MSISDN to Functional Number (FN), delete existing assignments, or display information on existing assignments. This allows flexible handling of MSISDN to FN assignments, accommodating the fact that the assignments of FNs to MSISDNs are only temporary.

Note: Registration and deregistration can also be performed via a dispatcher terminal (see Registration and Registration and Deregistration of Dispatcher Terminals, Change of Dispatcher Terminal Registration and Forced Deregistration Initiated by a Dispatcher Terminal).

Registration

Usually, users register to an FN at the beginning of their shift on a certain train or in a certain team, where they work in a certain function.

Deregistration

At the end of the shift when they cease to work in the respective function, they deregister themselves, making the FN available for the next person working in the respective function.

Forced deregistration

Authorized users can also deregister other users from an FN via USSD (forced deregistration). The deregistered user receives a notification in form of a USSD string. This function is typically used if a user forgets to deregister himself after the shift and the new responsible person wants to register himself.

Interrogation

Finally, users may also enquire about the registered MSISDN for a given FN or about which FNs are assigned to their own MSISDN.

Interrogation is performed via USSD access codes from the mobile phone or a cab radio.

Bulk operations

supports bulk operations for registration and deregistration procedures, that is, a cab radio or the lead driver can register/deregister for/from up to 10 FNs using only one USSD string. The implementation of this feature is optional.

Error handling

The user receives feedback for every event that occurs when dealing with the service. From the user‘s point of view, this event can be positive (e.g. successful registration of an FN) or negative (e.g. incorrect FN).

The feedback is obtained in form of a short text displayed on the mobile phone.
